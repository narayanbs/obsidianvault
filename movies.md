# Dilip kumar

If you're looking for the films that best represent Dilip Kumar among his finest:

1. **Mughal-e-Azam (1960)**
   His portrayal of Prince Salim is restrained yet powerful. The film is one of the greatest epics in Indian cinema and co-stars Madhubala and Prithviraj Kapoor.

2. **Devdas (1955)**
   Often regarded as the definitive screen version of Sarat Chandra Chattopadhyay's tragic hero. This performance cemented his "Tragedy King" image.

3. **Naya Daur (1957)**
   A classic drama about tradition versus modernization, with one of his most charismatic performances.

4. **Madhumati (1958)**
   A landmark romantic mystery with reincarnation as its central theme. It influenced many later Indian films.

5. **Ganga Jamuna (1961)**
   Produced by Dilip Kumar himself, this is considered one of his greatest performances. He plays a villager whose life takes a tragic turn.

6. **Aan (1952)**
   One of India's earliest Technicolor spectacles and a huge commercial success, showcasing him in a swashbuckling role.

7. **Andaz (1949)**
   A landmark romantic drama starring Dilip Kumar alongside Raj Kapoor and Nargis.

8. **Kohinoor (1960)**
   A lighthearted musical that showed his comic timing and versatility, a departure from his tragic roles.

9. **Ram Aur Shyam (1967)**
   His famous double role as timid Ram and spirited Shyam is one of the best dual performances in Hindi cinema.

10. **Shakti (1982)**
    A gripping father-son drama opposite Amitabh Bachchan. Many critics consider it one of the finest performances by both actors.

### If you're new to Dilip Kumar

A great viewing order would be:

* **Devdas** – for his classic tragic acting.
* **Naya Daur** – for his mass appeal and screen presence.
* **Mughal-e-Azam** – for the grandeur of classic Hindi cinema.
* **Ganga Jamuna** – for what many consider his career-best performance.
* **Shakti** – to see him in a mature role opposite Amitabh Bachchan.

These films together show why he is remembered not just as a major star, but as one of the most influential actors in the history of Indian cinema.
