
---

### 1️⃣ Original equation: squared length of a vector

$$
\langle \vec{CO} + t \vec{D}, \vec{CO} + t \vec{D} \rangle = r^2
$$

---

### 2️⃣ First expansion using distributive property

$$
\langle \vec{CO} + t \vec{D}, \vec{CO} \rangle
+ \langle \vec{CO} + t \vec{D}, t \vec{D} \rangle = r^2
$$

---

### 3️⃣ Expand each term using linearity

$$
\langle \vec{CO}, \vec{CO} \rangle
+ t \langle \vec{D}, \vec{CO} \rangle
+ t \langle \vec{CO}, \vec{D} \rangle
+ t^2 \langle \vec{D}, \vec{D} \rangle = r^2
$$

---

### 4️⃣ Combine symmetric terms (since $\langle \vec{D}, \vec{CO} \rangle = \langle \vec{CO}, \vec{D} \rangle$)

$$
\langle \vec{CO}, \vec{CO} \rangle
+ 2 t \langle \vec{CO}, \vec{D} \rangle
+ t^2 \langle \vec{D}, \vec{D} \rangle = r^2
$$
